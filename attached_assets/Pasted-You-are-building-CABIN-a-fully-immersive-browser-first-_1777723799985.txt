WHAT CABIN IS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Cabin is a diegetic, first-person productivity environment styled as a long-haul business class seat. The user sits inside a photorealistic aircraft cabin rendered in Three.js/WebGL. There is no traditional UI — every productivity interaction happens by clicking on physical objects inside the cabin. The IFE screen IS the dashboard. The notebook IS the note editor. The tray table IS the task surface. The engine hum IS the focus timer.

The entire experience runs gate-to-gate: you board, taxi, take off, cruise (this is deep work time), land, and arrive. Session length = flight time. Pomodoro sprints = flight legs.

THE 3D CABIN SCENE (CabinScene.js + CabinGeometry.js)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Build a first-person business class seat in Three.js. The camera represents the user's eyes, seated in the chair. The user can pan by dragging (mouse) or swiping (touch). Pan range: ±60° horizontal, ±30° vertical from center. No full rotation.

MATERIALS (use MeshStandardMaterial throughout, PBR):
- Seat shell: deep charcoal (#1a1a20), slight roughness 0.7, metalness 0.1
- Walnut tray table: warm brown (#6b4226), roughness 0.8, metalness 0.0, faint wood grain (use procedural UV noise)
- Faux marble console: off-white (#d4cfc8) with subtle grey vein lines (use canvas texture), roughness 0.3, metalness 0.05
- IFE bezel: matte black (#0d0d0f), roughness 0.9
- IFE screen: emissive panel (emissive color driven by current mode)
- Overhead bin plastic: cool grey (#b0adb8), roughness 0.6
- Carpet aisle: deep navy/charcoal pattern (#1e1e2a), roughness 1.0
- Window frame: brushed aluminum (#8a8a9a), metalness 0.7, roughness 0.4
- Duvet: quilted charcoal (#2a2a30) on ottoman (bottom of scene, partially visible)
- Amenity kit: small cream pouch (#e8e0d5) on the side console

GEOMETRY (build procedurally, no external GLB files):
- Seat shell: BoxGeometry + CylinderGeometry headrest, seat pan, armrests
- Tray table: BoxGeometry, hinged at the bottom of the IFE column. Stowed = flat against column. Deployed = horizontal, sliding out with animation.
- IFE: Large BoxGeometry panel, 16:10 aspect ratio. Screen face = PlaneGeometry with emissive material.
- Side console: BoxGeometry with slight bevel (use EdgesGeometry for visual crispness), faux marble material
- Capacitive buttons on console: small CylinderGeometry discs (4 total: Upright, Lounge, Bed, Lumbar)
- Storage cubby: BoxGeometry with a sliding door (AnimationMixer or manual lerp). Inside: a small cylinder for Evian bottle (light blue #c8e6f5), a hook with headphone silhouette (torus + cylinders).
- Overhead bin: large curved BoxGeometry above camera. Include gasper (air vent): small CylinderGeometry. Seatbelt sign: PlaneGeometry with canvas texture showing orange LED glow when active.
- Window: to the left of the seat (or right, depending on seat selection). Frame = BoxGeometry border. Window glass = PlaneGeometry with transparency + WindowView sky texture applied.
- Aisle: extends right (or left) of the seat. Floor is visible. Far end fades into darkness.

LIGHTING:
- DirectionalLight (main): warm white (#fff5e0), intensity driven by flight phase (boarding=1.2 cool white, taxi=0.9 blue-purple, cruise=0.6 warm, descent=1.0 warm orange, landing=1.2 back to white)
- AmbientLight: low intensity (0.3), matches phase color
- PointLight inside cubby: soft warm (#ffe4a0), intensity 0.4 (simulates cubby backlight)
- IFE screen emissive light: PointLight in front of IFE, color = current IFE mode color, low intensity 0.2
- Window bounce: subtle HemisphereLight (sky=current sky color, ground=#1a1a20, intensity 0.15)

Animate lighting transitions between phases with smooth lerping (2-second transitions).

Turbulence shake: small random offsets (max ±0.5°) applied via noise function during cruise phase.

The session is the core game loop. All phases are driven by a real JavaScript timer.

FLIGHT PHASES:
1. ONBOARDING: Seat selection + route selection. No timer yet. User configures session.
2. BOARDING (5 min fixed): Jet bridge visible, bright cabin lights, boarding music-style audio ambience.
3. TAXI (user-configurable 5-15 min): Tarmac view, cabin dims to moody blue-purple. Seatbelt sign ON.
4. TAKEOFF (3 min fixed): Engine spool-up audio, camera tilts upward, ground rushes away.
5. CRUISE (user's deep work time, configurable 30 min to 8 hours): Main session. Pomodoro cycles run here.
6. DESCENT (15 min fixed): Cabin brightens, IFE map shows approach, "prepare for arrival" PA.
7. LANDING (5 min fixed): Gear-down sound, ground rush, final deceleration.
8. ARRIVED: Session complete screen on IFE. Stats shown.

POMODORO INSIDE CRUISE:
- Sprint duration: configurable (default 25 min)
- Break duration: configurable (default 5 min)
- During sprint: Big Clock mode auto-activates on IFE. Engine hum steady.
- At sprint end: chime plays, break animation triggers (stand and walk OR tea fades onto tray).
- After break: new sprint begins automatically.

AUDIO ENGINE (AudioEngine.js)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

All audio via Web Audio API (no audio files, synthesized or tone-generated). AudioContext must start on first user gesture.

Sounds to implement:
1. Engine hum: continuous OscillatorNode (sawtooth wave ~90Hz + ~180Hz), gain driven by phase (0 during boarding, ramps to max during cruise, fades on landing). Add BiquadFilterNode (lowpass ~400Hz) for that muffled cabin sound.
2. Engine spool-up: during takeoff, frequency ramp from 60Hz → 150Hz over 20 seconds.
3. Seatbelt chime: two-tone pleasant chime (sine waves, 880Hz + 1108Hz, 0.3s each, fading out). Plays when seatbelt sign toggles.
4. Tray table clunk: short noise burst (BufferSourceNode with random noise, bandpass filtered ~200-800Hz, 0.15s). Plays on deploy/stow.
5. Button click: soft click (noise burst, 5ms, high frequency).
6. Cubby slide: smooth noise whoosh (filtered noise, 0.3s).
7. Meal service clink: high-pitched tink (sine, 1200Hz, 0.1s decay).
8. PA announcement: use SpeechSynthesisUtterance (Web Speech API) for text-to-speech announcements:
   - Boarding: "Ladies and gentlemen, welcome aboard. Flight attendants, doors to automatic and crosscheck."
   - Takeoff: "Cabin crew, please prepare for takeoff."
   - Cruise: "Ladies and gentlemen, the captain has turned off the fasten seatbelt sign. You are free to move about the cabin."
   - Descent: "Ladies and gentlemen, we are beginning our descent. Please return to your seats and fasten your seatbelts."
   - Landing: "Flight attendants, prepare for arrival."
9. Walking sounds: during break aisle walk, subtle footstep rhythm (noise bursts every 0.5s).
10. Galley ambience: during break, soft low-volume chatter (filtered noise with slow amplitude modulation).

- Audio: quiet ambient hum, distant boarding music (slow LFO modulated tone), then PA announcement
- IFE shows: welcome screen with airline logo and route info
- User can still configure tasks during this phase

PHASE 2 — TAXI (user-configured time)
- Jet bridge animates retracting (lerp away)
- Window: tarmac texture scrolling slowly left
- Cabin lights: dim to moody blue-purple (lerp DirectionalLight color + intensity)
- Seatbelt sign: turns ON (canvas texture on overhead sign updates)
- Camera: slight side-to-side sway (simulates taxi bumps)
- Audio: engine at low frequency, tire rumble noise

PHASE 3 — TAKEOFF (3 min)
- Window: tarmac scrolling faster, then blur as speed increases, then sky
- Camera: pitch tilts upward +12°
- Cabin: G-force simulation — loose items (amenity kit) shift slightly
- Audio: engine spool-up, PA announcement, seatbelt chime
- After 90 seconds of takeoff: transition to CRUISE phase

PHASE 4 — CRUISE (session duration)
- Window: sky view (time-of-day driven)
- Seatbelt sign: turns OFF after 3 minutes, chime plays
- Pomodoro cycle begins
- Occasional turbulence (camera shake, seatbelt sign briefly flickers on)
- Meal service events trigger on schedule
- User does their actual work (tasks, notes, IFE timer)

PHASE 5 — BREAK (every sprint end, 5 min)
- Option A (Stand & Walk): Camera animates down aisle toward galley, 3D aisle extends forward. Galley glimpsed. After 5 min, camera walks back.
- Option B (Stay Seated): A tea cup + saucer fades onto the tray table. Gentle steam particle animation.
- The user chooses at the start by a small diegetic prompt on the IFE: "Stretch your legs?" [Walk to Galley] [Stay Seated]

PHASE 6 — DESCENT (15 min)
- IFE map auto-switches to approach view
- Window: nose tilts down, ground appears (terrain texture at bottom)
- Cabin lights: brighten back to white
- PA: "prepare for arrival" announcement
- Seatbelt sign: turns back ON
- Audio: gentle engine wind-down, pressure change hiss

PHASE 7 — LANDING (5 min)
- Window: ground rush, runway approach
- Camera shake: heavier landing shake
- Landing gear sound (timed noise burst)
- Reverse thrust audio (high-frequency rumble)
- Deceleration: camera pitch slightly forward
- Final PA: "Ladies and gentlemen, welcome to [destination]. Local time is [time]. Thank you for flying with us."

PHASE 8 — ARRIVED
- Cabin lights full white
- Jet bridge re-extends to window
- IFE shows session summary:
  - Total flight time
  - Sprints completed
  - Tasks done / total tasks
  - Notes written (character count)
  - "Productivity altitude": a fun metric
- "Deplane" button resets the app to onboarding

SEAT PERSPECTIVE SYSTEM
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

The camera position and the visible geometry change based on seat selection:

1A (True Left Window): Window is directly to the left. Aisle is behind the console to the right. Maximally private feel — console fills right side.
2B (Left Aisle): Window is further left, partially blocked by console. Aisle is immediately to the right. More open.
1D (Center-Left Aisle): Aisle to immediate left. Console in center between you and 2D seat. Window is far left.
2D (Center-Left Center/"Honeymoon"): Tucked in center. Console blocks aisle. No direct window view — only overhead light.
2G (Center-Right Center): Mirror of 2D.
1G (Center-Right Aisle): Mirror of 1D.
2J (Right Aisle): Mirror of 2B.
1L (True Right Window): Mirror of 1A.

Implement this as a camera position + rotation preset per seat, with the geometry arrangement changing accordingly. The window plane is repositioned left or right. The console is repositioned. The aisle appears on the correct side.

PERFORMANCE & POLISH
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

- Target 60fps. Use renderer.setPixelRatio(Math.min(devicePixelRatio, 1.5)) to cap pixel ratio.
- Frustum culling on all objects (default Three.js behavior — keep meshes in scene only when needed).
- All textures are canvas-generated (no external image fetches for 3D assets).
- requestAnimationFrame loop drives both the Three.js scene and window view canvas.
- Passive event listeners for scroll/touch to avoid jank.
- Reduce motion: if prefers-reduced-motion, disable camera shake and break walk animation.
- Low-power fallback: detect if frame time > 33ms (< 30fps) and reduce cloud animation complexity.
